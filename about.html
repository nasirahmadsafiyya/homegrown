Welcome to HomeGrown Harvest

The history of farmers in Nigeria dates back thousands of years. The earliest known agricultural practices in the region can be traced back to the indigenous people who practiced subsistence farming. These early farmers cultivated crops such as millet, sorghum, yam, and cowpea, as well as raising livestock such as cattle, goats, and sheep.
With the introduction of iron tools by the Nok culture around 1000 BCE, farming techniques improved, leading to increased productivity. The rise of these early agrarian communities gave birth to various farming systems, including shifting cultivation, mixed farming, and settled farming.

Healthy Eating Healthy Life

Farm produce plays a crucial role in providing people with nutritious and sustainable food options. It also contributes to the economic development of rural areas and promotes environmental sustainability through responsible farming practices.

The farm-to-table movement has gained popularity in recent years, promoting locally sourced and fresh farm produce in restaurants and households. This movement aims to reduce the carbon footprint associated with long-distance transportation of food and support local farmers and economies.
